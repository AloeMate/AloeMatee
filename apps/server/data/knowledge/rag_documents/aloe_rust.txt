DISEASE: Aloe Rust
SCIENTIFIC NAME: Phakopsora pachyrhizi (related rust fungus affecting succulents)
CATEGORY: Fungal Disease
AFFECTS: Aloe vera leaves (primarily older, lower leaves)

SYMPTOMS:
- Orange, yellow, or brown powdery pustules on leaf surfaces
- Pustules break open to release rusty-coloured spores
- Infected leaves turn yellow, then brown and die
- Spores spread easily by wind and water splash
- Typically starts on lower leaves and moves upward
- Leaves may shrivel and drop prematurely

CAUSES:
- Fungal pathogen in humid, warm conditions (20-30°C)
- Poor air circulation around the plant
- Overhead watering or water remaining on leaves
- Overcrowded pots or growing areas
- Introduction of infected plants nearby

SCIENTIFIC TREATMENT:
Step 1 - Isolate immediately: Move the plant away from other plants to prevent spore spread. Rust spores are airborne and highly contagious.
Step 2 - Remove infected leaves: Using sterilized scissors (wiped with 70% isopropyl alcohol), remove all leaves with orange pustules. Seal in plastic bag and dispose in household waste - never compost.
Step 3 - Apply sulfur-based fungicide: Spray all remaining leaf surfaces with wettable sulfur (2g/L water) or copper oxychloride solution. Cover both upper and lower leaf surfaces thoroughly. Apply in the early morning when temperature is below 30°C.
Step 4 - Repeat fungicide treatment: Apply every 7-10 days for 3-4 consecutive treatments. Continue even after visible symptoms disappear to prevent recurrence.
Step 5 - Improve air circulation: Repot into a larger container with more spacing. Ensure good ventilation. Use a small fan indoors.
Step 6 - Change watering habits: Water only at soil level, never overhead. Water in the morning so leaves dry quickly. Allow soil to dry between waterings.

AYURVEDIC / NATURAL TREATMENT:
Step 1 - Remove infected tissue: Remove affected leaves as described above.
Step 2 - Neem oil spray: Mix 2 tablespoons cold-pressed neem oil with 1 teaspoon castile soap in 1 litre warm water. Shake well and spray on all leaf surfaces. Neem oil has natural antifungal properties and disrupts the spore lifecycle. Apply every 5-7 days.
Step 3 - Turmeric paste: Mix 1 tablespoon turmeric powder with 1 tablespoon coconut oil into a paste. Apply directly on pustule areas. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) contains curcumin which has proven antifungal activity.
Step 4 - Garlic spray: Blend 4-5 garlic cloves in 500ml water, strain, dilute 1:5 with water. Spray on leaves. Allicin in garlic has natural antifungal properties.
Step 5 - Improve soil health: Add vermicompost to soil to strengthen plant immunity. Healthy soil microbiome naturally suppresses fungal pathogens.

DOSAGE AND FREQUENCY:
- Sulfur fungicide: Every 7-10 days, maximum 6 applications per season
- Neem oil: Every 5-7 days until symptoms clear, then monthly as prevention
- Do not apply any spray when temperature exceeds 35°C (risk of leaf burn)

SAFETY WARNINGS:
- Always wear gloves and eye protection when applying fungicide
- Sulfur and copper compounds are toxic to aquatic organisms - keep away from drains and ponds
- Do not apply fungicide on the same day as neem oil - wait 48 hours between different treatments
- Wash hands thoroughly after handling diseased plant material
- Dispose of infected leaves in sealed bags with household waste - NEVER compost

PREVENTION:
- Inspect new plants for rust pustules before introducing to your collection
- Maintain spacing of at least 30cm between aloe plants
- Water at soil level only, never wet the leaves
- Ensure good air circulation at all times
- Apply preventive neem oil spray once per month during humid seasons

WHEN TO CONSULT AN EXPERT:
- Rust spreads to more than 50% of the plant within one week
- Multiple plants develop rust simultaneously
- Symptoms do not improve after 3-4 fungicide applications
- Plant shows additional symptoms: root rot, stem collapse, or severe wilting
- You are unsure how to safely use fungicide chemicals
